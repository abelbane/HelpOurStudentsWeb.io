Donations.

Effective September 1, 2022, each recipient receives $150 each month, from September through June, for a total of $1,500. The Help Our Students Program is a not-for-profit organization, registered as a charity under the Income Tax Act (Canada). 100% of all donations are distributed to students who are selected by their school. Your donations are never used to pay administrative costs which are paid by the volunteer Board of Directors. Donors receive an annual President’s Report. Each President’s Report contains a financial statement and a link to the recipients’ letters describing how the Help Our Students Program has impacted their lives.

There are many ways to donate to the Help Our Students Program:
Directly by cheque or etransfer
Online by credit card, debit card or PayPal
By donating shares.
You can specify which school you wish to support or let us use your donation where it’s most needed.
You can make a donation in honour of someone (e.g. your child’s teacher) or in memory of a loved one. When you choose to make a dedicated donation, we will send a donation card to you or to the address you specify.

Online.

Click on the Donate NOW button. You will be transferred to the CanadaHelps website, a Canadian registered charity that processes online donations for Canadian charities. Once you donate, you will receive your receipt for income tax purposes directly from CanadaHelps. You may make a one-time donation or a monthly, recurring donation that you may stop at any time. Please note that CanadaHelps deducts 4.0% of each donation (3.5% for monthly donations) to cover their processing and administrative costs. Once we receive the remaining amount, all of it is distributed to students.

Shares & Securities.

You can donate shares in Canadian equities. When you donate shares to a charitable organization, you receive an official receipt for tax purposes for the amount of the sale of the shares and you are exempt from paying tax on any capital gains incurred as a result of the sale. The CanadaHelps fee for processing securities varies between 2% - 3%, depending on the value of the securities. For more information on donating securities, click here to visit the Canada Revenue Agency.

Insurance.

By donating a new or existing life insurance policy, you can make a large donation that is greater than the premiums paid. By naming the Help Our Students Program as the beneficiary, your annual premiums qualify for charitable tax receipts.

Legacy Giving.

By donating a new or existing life insurance policy, you can make a large donation that is greater than the premiums paid. By naming the Help Our Students Program as the beneficiary, your annual premiums qualify for charitable tax receipts.
